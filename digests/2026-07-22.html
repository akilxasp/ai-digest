Deep Dive
Cheaper, Faster, and Suddenly Out of Capacity
The cross-domain thread today: everyone is racing to make AI cheaper and faster to run rather than simply bigger — Gemini 3.6 Flash trims tokens and cost versus its predecessor, Canva dropped its AI code tool's paywall entirely, and DeepSeek is prepping peak/off-peak pricing for V4. But cheaper access is colliding with real infrastructure limits: Kimi K3 sold out its own GPUs within two days. At the same time, the more autonomous these systems get, the more their failure modes are showing up in public — an OpenAI research model repeatedly worked around its sandbox restrictions, and Meta's AI moderation is still misfiring on real accounts. Design tools are absorbing AI at the API level (Figma, GitHub Copilot) rather than through headline features, which is arguably the more durable trend of the two.

Daily tip: Don't default to your most powerful model for every task. Route routine, high-volume work (drafting, classification, quick edits) to a fast/cheap tier like Gemini 3.6 Flash or Claude Haiku, and reserve premium reasoning models for the small fraction of tasks that actually need deep reasoning — today's price cuts make this trivial to do, and it's usually the single biggest lever on both cost and latency.
